8-Week Beginner Program

FORGE YOUR BODY

A science-backed, full-body muscle plan built for men over 40. 3–4 days a week. 50–65 minutes per session.

Who This Is For

  • Men aged 35–55, beginner or returning to training
  • Wants to build muscle and improve overall shape
  • Can commit to 3–4 gym sessions per week
  • No injury that prevents basic compound movements
  • Wants a plan simple enough to follow alone in a gym

How to Use This Plan

  • Follow the Weekly Schedule tab for your training days
  • Tap a workout day to expand it; check off exercises as you go
  • Always complete the warm-up before lifting
  • Rest at least 1 full day between sessions
  • Follow the Progression rules in the Progress tab each week
  • Tap any video link to see how the exercise is performed
  • Use substitutions listed when a machine is taken

⚠️ Safety Rules — Read First

  • Always warm up — never skip it at 40+
  • Use weights you can control for the full rep range before adding weight
  • Stop if you feel sharp pain, not just muscle burn
  • Progress slowly — the goal of Week 1 is to learn form, not go heavy
  • If a machine is taken, use the substitution listed on the exercise card
  • Sleep 7–8 hours — recovery is when you grow, not in the gym
  • Check with your doctor before starting if you have any pre-existing conditions

8-Week Model PROGRESSION

The Golden Rule

Complete all prescribed reps with good form before adding weight. Never sacrifice form for weight. At 40+, tendons and joints adapt slower than muscles — be patient and progressive.

The +2 Rep / +Weight Rule

Each week, try to either:

  • Add 1–2 extra reps to at least one set, OR
  • Once you can do all sets at the top of the rep range, add the smallest weight increment available
  • For dumbbells: jump from 12.5kg to 15kg when ready
  • For machines: move up one plate/peg

8-Week Roadmap

WeeksFocus & Goal
1–2Learn form. Go lighter than you think you need. Focus on feeling the right muscles. Every rep should be controlled.
3–4Find your true working weights. By end of Week 4, sets should feel like 7–8/10 difficulty on last rep.
5–6Add weight or reps. You should be stronger than Week 1. Track weights used in notes app or notebook.
7–8Push harder. Final week should feel challenging. After Week 8, take a deload (lighter week) before starting again.

Deload Week (After Week 8)

  • Keep the same exercises
  • Reduce weight by 30–40%
  • Reduce sets to 2 per exercise
  • This lets joints, tendons, and nervous system recover before the next 8 weeks
  • Don't skip deloads — they are why men over 40 stay injury-free

🥩 Protein — The Most Important Rule

  • Target: 1.8–2g of protein per kg of bodyweight daily
  • Example: 85kg man = ~160–170g protein per day
  • Best sources: Chicken breast, eggs, Greek yogurt, tuna, cottage cheese, beef, salmon
  • Whey protein shake is helpful but not mandatory
  • Spread protein across 4 meals — your body uses protein better this way
  • Without enough protein, muscle growth stalls no matter how hard you train

😴 Sleep — Your Actual Growth Time

  • You don't grow in the gym — you grow while you sleep
  • 7–9 hours is the target range; 8 is ideal
  • Testosterone drops sharply with under 6 hours — critical for men over 40
  • Prioritise consistent sleep time (same bedtime, same wake time)
  • Dark room, cool temperature, no screens 30 min before bed

💧 Hydration

  • Aim for 2.5–3 litres of water per day
  • Drink 500ml before you go to the gym
  • Sip during training — don't chug large amounts at once
  • Urine should be pale yellow — dark yellow = dehydrated

🥗 Eating Strategy (Keep It Simple)

  • To build muscle: eat at or slightly above maintenance (200–300 kcal surplus)
  • Don't overeat — excess fat gain slows your progress and motivation
  • Fill half your plate with protein + vegetables
  • Fill the rest with complex carbs (rice, oats, sweet potato, bread)
  • Don't fear carbs — they fuel your workouts
  • Eat within 1–2 hours after training for best recovery

Supplement Priority List

  • 1. Creatine Monohydrate — 3–5g daily, backed by decades of research, safe and effective for muscle and strength
  • 2. Whey Protein — only if you can't hit protein targets from food
  • 3. Vitamin D3 + K2 — important for men over 40, supports testosterone and bone health
  • Everything else is secondary. Don't buy fat burners, test boosters, or expensive pre-workouts.

Men Over 40 — Special Notes

  • Recovery takes longer than your 20s — this is normal, not a limitation
  • Don't train through sharp joint pain — get it assessed
  • Consider getting testosterone and Vitamin D levels checked annually
  • Stress management matters — high cortisol (stress hormone) actively fights muscle growth
  • Consistency over intensity: showing up 3 days a week for 12 months beats 6 days for 6 weeks every time
